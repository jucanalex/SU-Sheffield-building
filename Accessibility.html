Accessibility

We have tackled a series of accessibility issues like having photo alternative text for blind people using a screen reader. We have a color scheme that will not influence epileptic seizures. Our content is easy to access and read. We have big and easy clickable buttons, nobody can miss-click them.

"We incorporate website accessibility standards into the design and maintenance of our website to ensure accessibility for all people regardless of ability or method of access.We've been using the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.0 from the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C).These recommendations outline how to make website content accessible to a wider range of people with disabilities. "[1]

Help Us improve

If for any reason you cannot access any of the material on our website,please contact us either by using the Contact page or directly by emailing Antohi Vasile (vantohi1@sheffield.ac.uk) or Alexandru Jucan (ajucan1@sheffield.ac.uk) and we will work to resolve the problem as soon as possible.
